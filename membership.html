Student

Free. No barriers. No qualification required.

Student membership is open to anyone actively studying project management. Activate learners join automatically. There are no fees and no conditions.

Student membership builds the IPP community from the ground up. The path to Associate is automatic once you qualify.

  • Digital student badge for LinkedIn and email signature
  • IPP community forum and peer study groups
  • Resource library: templates, reading lists, glossary
  • Early access to IPP events and webinars
  • Discounted path to Associate on qualification

Associate · AIPP

Your first verified credential.

AIPP is the natural next step after completing your foundation qualification. The designation is verifiable by employers in the public directory and sits credibly on a LinkedIn profile.

At £29 it is a straightforward decision for anyone who has just passed a qualification and wants something lasting to show for it.

  • AIPP designation: verifiable by employers instantly

Member · MIPP

The practising professional tier.

Member status is for practising project professionals with at least two years of active delivery experience. MIPP is a meaningful credential: peer-reviewed entry requirements mean it carries weight with employers who understand it.

Calibrate access is included. For members who would otherwise pay for Calibrate separately, the economics are straightforward.

  • MIPP designation: verified and employer-searchable
  • Calibrate full access included in membership fee
  • Priority directory listing with competency profile
  • Peer review and mentoring network
  • IPP CPD event programme access
  • Voting rights in IPP governance
Apply for Member status

Fellow · FIPP

Senior, peer-reviewed status.

Fellow status is for senior project professionals with significant experience and a demonstrable contribution to the profession. FIPP is not self-declared seniority: it is peer-reviewed and evidence-based. That distinction matters to the people who apply for it.

Fellows are invited to sit on IPP working groups, assessment panels, and governance committees. They contribute to the standards that define the profession.

  • FIPP designation: premium directory listing with Fellow badge
  • Invitation to governance and standards working groups
  • Input into qualification development
  • Recognition at IPP events and in publications
  • Full Calibrate access included
