CPD and professional development

Structured learning that counts
towards APM CPD.

The APM framework for continuing professional development uses five questions: where am I now, where do I want to be, how will I get there, how am I doing, and what next. Module Six helps individuals answer all five clearly and with evidence.

Completing Module Six constitutes formal structured learning under the APM CPD framework. Each topic completed, each practice question answered, and each module checkpoint passed is a recorded, evidenced learning activity that can be logged directly against a CPD record or personal development plan.

For organisations where staff maintain APM membership or are working towards APMP and beyond, Module Six provides a documented starting point. Progress reports are exportable and give individuals the dates, topics, and assessment scores they need to complete their CPD log entries accurately.

Inclusion and accessibility

A learning tool that works for
every person on your team.

Project teams are diverse. Module Six is built to reflect that. Every learner gets the same content and the same qualification outcome — what changes is how it is delivered. Learners choose the experience that works for them, without having to declare anything or ask for adjustments.

Focus mode

Delivers content one section at a time with no sidebars, no competing links, and a clear progress indicator throughout. Designed with ADHD and anxiety in mind, but chosen by learners of all kinds who prefer a linear, distraction-free path.

OpenDyslexic font

Learners can switch to OpenDyslexic at any time from their profile. The setting persists across sessions and devices. No forms, no disclosure, no separate process — just a preference a learner can set and forget.

Background tints

Five background colour options — white, cream, yellow, peach, and pale blue — reduce the visual stress of reading on screen. Particularly useful for learners with Irlen syndrome or visual processing differences.

Audio summaries

Every module has a spoken summary — two to three minutes of clearly narrated key points. Learners who process information better through listening can use audio as their primary revision tool, not an afterthought.

Self-paced, no time pressure

Learners move at their own pace with no countdown timers, no session limits, and no performance warnings in learning mode. The mock exam is timed because the real exam is — everything else is fully self-directed.

WCAG 2.1 AA compliant

Both the web app and phone PWA are built to WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards — keyboard navigation, screen reader support, sufficient colour contrast, and no reliance on colour alone to convey meaning.

Common questions

What L&D teams usually ask.

Does completing Module Six count as formal CPD?
Yes. Completing structured online learning is a recognised CPD activity under the APM framework. Learners can record completed topics and modules in their APM CPD log with dates and assessment scores as supporting evidence.
Can progress records be used in appraisals or competency reviews?
Yes. Topic-level progress and readiness scores give line managers concrete, evidenced data for performance reviews, PDPs, and competency framework assessments. Progress data can be exported as CSV for use in your own systems.
Is there a minimum order?
The Team tier starts at five seats. If you need fewer, get in touch and we can discuss options.
What happens when a learner finishes?
Their account stays active until the licence expires. They can revisit the revision hub, retake the mock exam, and access their full progress record for the remainder of the 12 months.
How does seat reassignment work?
If a team member leaves, reassign their seat to a new starter from the admin dashboard. The new learner inherits the remaining time on that seat. All seats in an order expire on the same date.
Is the content aligned to the APM competency framework?
The content is built from APM source material and covers the full PFQ syllabus, which maps to the APM competency framework at foundation level. It is the right starting point for practitioners working towards APMP and beyond.
Do you offer invoicing?
Yes. All team orders are invoiced. We accept BACS on 30-day terms and include a PO number field on the invoice.
How long does setup take?
Once an order is confirmed, access codes are issued within one working day. Learners can start immediately after redeeming their code. No IT configuration required.
What data do you hold on learners?
Name, work email, and progress data. Data is stored securely and is not shared with third parties. Full details are in our privacy policy.
What if we need SSO or more than 99 seats?
Get in touch. SSO is available for larger orders alongside custom reporting and dedicated onboarding support.
